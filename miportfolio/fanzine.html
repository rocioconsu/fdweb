The Price of Growing Up

A critical look at adulthood through The Little Prince

The price of growing up is a commemorative fanzine created for the 100th anniversary of The Little Prince. This project offers a critical and contemporary reinterpretation of Antoine de Saint-Exupéry’s classic, focusing on the adult world and the habits we often adopt without realizing: meaningless power, forgetting ourselves, living in routine, or using distractions to escape reality.

Each section of the fanzine is based on one of the characters the Little Prince meets during his journey — the king, the drinker, the businessman, among others — and connects their attitude in the book to present-day issues. Short reflections accompany each part, inviting the reader to think about these behaviors in a personal and introspective way.

The visual language of the fanzine is built with collage illustrations, a technique that adds a fragmented and critical tone to the work. The color palette — blue, black, grey, and white — creates a calm, poetic, and slightly melancholic mood, in line with the message.

The Price of Growing Up is more than a tribute; it is an invitation to question what we may have lost on the way to becoming adults. A project that blends literature, reflection, and editorial design to recover the voice of the child we once were — and might still be able to hear.
