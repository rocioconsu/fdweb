Magma

Structure, rhythm, and typographic personality

Magma is an experimental typeface developed from a geometric grid, created as an exercise in form and structure exploration. Each letter features rounded corners and a distinctive cut on one of its edges, giving the typeface a unique rhythm and strong identity. The result is a typographic system that combines structural precision with expressive details.

The project is presented through a typographic poster that works as both a showcase and a visual summary. On a black and pink background, the composition highlights the contrast between geometric discipline and creative freedom. The original grid remains visible in the background, showing the technical process behind each letter and adding an educational dimension to the piece.

Magma is not just a typeface — it’s a reflection on the balance between structure and visual freedom. Its design offers a contemporary, versatile, and recognizable option, suitable for editorial use or more expressive graphic projects. The poster communicates this dual nature: control and disruption, order and personality.
