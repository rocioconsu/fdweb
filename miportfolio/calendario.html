A Reinterpretation of Girl at the Window

This project is an illustrated calendar that pays tribute to Salvador Dalí’s painting Girl at the Window (1925). Through a modern reinterpretation, the image of the girl — seen from behind as she looks out of the window — becomes the main element of the calendar. The scene is illustrated using watercolors, which give a soft and emotional atmosphere, similar to Dalí’s original work but with a more personal and contemporary touch.

The watercolor illustration fills almost the entire calendar page, leaving only the right side for the calendar layout. Each month presents a different version of the girl at the window, showing changes that reflect the seasons or emotional moments during the year. In addition, every month includes a phrase from a song that talks about that specific month, creating a connection between the image, time, and music.

The calendar was completely designed using Adobe InDesign, which helped to create a clean and professional layout. This work is not only a useful calendar, but also an artistic and narrative piece that combines illustration, graphic design, and cultural references to offer a complete visual experience.
