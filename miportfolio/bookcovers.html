Book covers

Classic novels reimagined through color and symbolism

This project is a redesign of three classic book covers — Pride and Prejudice, Moby-Dick, and The Picture of Dorian Gray — created under a specific visual constraint: using only three colors, one for each book. Through a symbolic and minimalist approach, each cover captures the central conflict of the story using a single, meaningful object.

For Moby-Dick, a deep blue background frames a broken compass — a symbol of Captain Ahab’s obsession and lost direction — placed over a drawn outline of a whale in the background.

The Picture of Dorian Gray features an orange-red tone and a torn portrait revealing the true, aged version of Dorian beneath: a faded figure with grey hair and a serious, decayed expression — a clear metaphor for hidden moral decay.

Pride and Prejudice is represented in green, showing a crumpled letter with smudged ink, torn diagonally — evoking emotional conflict, miscommunication, and the unspoken tension that drives the plot.

All three covers share the same textured background for visual unity, while their distinct colors and symbols create strong individual identities. The result is a cohesive yet diverse series that offers a contemporary visual interpretation of literary classics through simplicity and symbolism.
